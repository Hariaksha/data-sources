**Theme:** Governance & Civil Liberties
**Source:** Sabin Center for Climate Change Law / Climate Science Legal Defense Fund
**Geographic Coverage:** United States (federal, state, and local)
**Time Coverage:** November 2016–present
**Unit of Observation:** Incident (government action)
**Format:** Web, downloadable (CSV)

## Description

Tracks reported U.S. government attempts to restrict or suppress scientific
research, education, communication, or the use of scientific information.
Entries are drawn from media reports and categorized into 7 action types:
Government Censorship, Self-Censorship, Budget Cuts, Personnel Changes,
Research Hindrance, Bias and Misrepresentation, and Interference with Education.
As of April 2026, the tracker contains 718+ incidents spanning federal and
state administrations from Trump (first and second terms) and others.
Relevant to research on science-policy conflict, environmental governance,
and the political economy of climate regulation.

## Variables

- `Date` — month/year of incident
- `Explanation` — action category (e.g., Budget Cuts, Personnel Changes)
- `Scientists Affected` — field(s) impacted (Climate, Public Health, Other)
- `Agency` — government body involved (Federal or State, with sub-agency)
- `Presidential Administration` — administration under which action occurred
- `Summary` — brief description of the incident
- `Update Status` — whether the entry has been updated since initial filing

## Merge Keys & Compatibility

- Filter by `Agency` to merge with **Fragile States Index** or **Freedom House** on U.S. state-level governance indicators
- Combine with **Academic Freedom Index** via administration/year for longitudinal science-freedom analysis
