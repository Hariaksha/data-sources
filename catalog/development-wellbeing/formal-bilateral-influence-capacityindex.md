# Formal Bilateral Influence Capacity (FBIC) Index

**Theme:** International Relations & Development
**Source:** Josef Korbel School of Global and Public Affairs, University of Denver
**Geographic Coverage:** Global (dyadic — country pairs)
**Time Coverage:** 1960 – recent (annual)
**Unit of Observation:** Country-dyad-year
**Format:** Downloadable dataset
**Access:** [FBIC – Pardee School](https://korbel.du.edu/pardee/formal-bilateral-influence-capacity/)
**License:** Free to access (academic)
**Last Verified:** April 2026

---

## Description

Measures the formal capacity of one country to influence another, based on trade dependence,
investment ties, and political agreements. A dyadic dataset — each observation is a pair of
countries in a given year, making it suitable for bilateral relationship analysis.

## Potential Research Questions

- Which country pairs have asymmetric influence relationships relevant to climate diplomacy?
- How does bilateral influence capacity correlate with joint climate commitments?

## Merge Keys & Compatibility

- Dyadic structure: merge on `iso3c_origin` + `iso3c_target` + `year`
- Unique in the catalog — most other indices are monadic (one row per country)
