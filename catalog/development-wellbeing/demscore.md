# DEMSCORE

## Overview

DEMSCORE (an acronym for **Dem**ocracy, **E**nvironment, **M**igration, **S**ocial policy, **Co**nflict, and **Re**presentation) is an innovative e-infrastructure providing free access to harmonized data from several of the world's largest datasets in the social sciences. It brings together leading research infrastructures to create a world-leading hub for contextual data with user-friendly access to data, documentation, and online visualization tools.

- **Website**: https://www.demscore.se/
- **GitHub**: https://github.com/demscore
- **Data Download Portal**: https://www.demscore.se/data/data-download-portal/

## Thematic Coverage

DEMSCORE integrates data across the following thematic domains:

- **Democracy** – political regimes, electoral processes, civil liberties
- **Environment** – environmental policy and sustainability indicators
- **Migration** – migration and refugee flows
- **Social Policy** – welfare states, social protection, labor market policies
- **Conflict** – armed conflict, organized violence, peace processes
- **Representation** – parliamentary representation, political parties, governments

## Data Access

Data can be downloaded through four selection methods:

1. **By Output Unit** – Select an output format and create a custom dataset, filtering by country, year, and source
2. **By Variable** – Browse the full database and download variables matching specific research interests
3. **By Codebook Section** – Retrieve all variables related to a topic organized by codebook section
4. **By Download ID** – Re-download a previously built dataset using a unique download ID

- **Download Interface**: https://www.demscore.se/data/data-download-portal/
- **Static Datasets**: https://www.demscore.se/data/static-datasets/

## FAIR Data Principles

DEMSCORE is committed to the FAIR principles for research data management:

- **Findable** – data is indexed and discoverable
- **Accessible** – free open access via web interface
- **Interoperable** – harmonized merging across partner datasets
- **Reusable** – documented with codebooks and methodology guides

More information: https://www.demscore.se/about/demscore/demscore-and-fair-data/

## Notes

- DEMSCORE addresses the persistent problem of inconsistent and incompatible datasets in the social sciences through functional data engineering principles enabling real-time, customized cross-dataset merging.
- The infrastructure supports large-scale comparative analyses relevant to the UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).
- The consortium is led by the University of Gothenburg and funded by the Swedish Research Council.
