# NOAA Billion-Dollar Weather and Climate Disasters

> **⚠️ Status Notice:** NOAA's National Centers for Environmental Information (NCEI) will no longer be updating this dataset, in alignment with evolving priorities, statutory mandates, and staffing changes. All past reports spanning **1980–2024** remain authoritative, archived, and publicly available. The dataset is now maintained and expanded by **[Climate Central](https://www.climatecentral.org/climate-services/billion-dollar-disasters)**.

---

## Overview

The **U.S. Billion-Dollar Weather and Climate Disasters** dataset, produced by NOAA NCEI, tracks all weather and climate events in the United States that have caused at least **$1 billion in total damages** (CPI-adjusted to the most recent year). It is one of the most comprehensive public records of large-scale climate-related economic losses in U.S. history.

- **Temporal coverage:** 1980 – 2024
- **Geographic scope:** United States
- **Update frequency:** Monthly (discontinued; final update covers through 2024)
- **DOI:** [10.25921/stkw-7w73](https://doi.org/10.25921/stkw-7w73)

---

## Disaster Types Tracked

The dataset classifies events into the following categories:

| Disaster Type         | Examples                                      |
|-----------------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| Tropical Cyclone      | Hurricanes, tropical storms                   |
| Severe Storm          | Tornadoes, hail, high winds, derechos         |
| Inland Flood          | River flooding, flash floods                  |
| Drought               | Agricultural and hydrological drought         |
| Wildfire              | Seasonal and individual wildland fire events  |
| Winter Storm          | Blizzards, ice storms, extreme cold           |
| Freeze                | Crop freezes, infrastructure damage           |
| Heat Wave             | Extreme heat events with mass casualties      |

---

## Key Statistics (1980–2024)

- **403+** billion-dollar disaster events recorded
- **$2.9+ trillion** in total CPI-adjusted damages
- **~17,000** direct and indirect deaths
- **2023** holds the record with **28 events** in a single year
- **2024** saw **27 events** totaling approximately **$182.7 billion**
- Every U.S. state has experienced at least one billion-dollar disaster

---

## Methodology

- **Cost threshold:** $1 billion (CPI-adjusted to current year using the Consumer Price Index)
- **Damage components included:** Property, infrastructure, agriculture, and insured losses; business interruption and supply-chain losses where applicable
- **Damage components excluded:** Environmental remediation, long-term health costs, and indirect macroeconomic effects
- **Inflation adjustment:** CPI is used (rather than GDP deflators) to allow consistent year-to-year comparison
- **Tracking start (1980):** Chosen due to consistent availability of National Weather Service damage assessments and insurance industry loss data

## Data Access & Download

The archived dataset is available via the official landing page:

- **Dataset Landing Page:** https://www.ncei.noaa.gov/access/metadata/landing-page/bin/iso?id=gov.noaa.nodc:0209268
- **data.gov mirror:** https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/u-s-billion-dollar-weather-and-climate-disasters-1980-present-ncei-accession-02092681
- **Format:** CSV, JSON (event-level and summary-level)

### Active Continuation (Post-2024)
Climate Central now maintains and expands the dataset with greater granularity:
- Events tracked down to **$100 million** in damage (vs. the $1 billion NOAA threshold)
- Individual wildfire tracking (vs. NOAA's seasonal aggregation)
- Access: https://www.climatecentral.org/climate-services/billion-dollar-disasters
