## Description

The most comprehensive global database of climate change litigation, tracking 4,800+
cases where climate change law, policy, or science is a material issue. Merges the
Sabin Center's previously separate U.S. and Global Climate Litigation Databases into
a single unified platform, built with NLP-powered search in partnership with Climate
Policy Radar. Covers lawsuits, administrative proceedings, and quasi-judicial actions
brought by and against governments, corporations, and individuals across all major
jurisdictions. Peer-reviewed by a network of 175 practitioners and scholars across
198 jurisdictions as of June 2025. Relevant to research on climate governance,
corporate accountability, fossil fuel litigation, and the political economy of
climate policy.

## Key Variables

- `Case name` — title of the case (e.g., plaintiff v. defendant)
- `Filing date` — year the case was filed
- `Status` — current procedural status or outcome
- `At issue` — brief description of the legal question(s) at stake
- `Case category` — thematic classification (e.g., Federal Statutory Claims, Constitutional Claims,
  Adaptation, Carbon Offsets and Credits, Climate Change Protesters and Scientists)
- `Principal law` — primary legal instrument invoked (e.g., Clean Air Act, Paris Agreement)
- `Jurisdiction` — court or tribunal (e.g., U.S. District Court, European Court of Human Rights)
- `Geography` — country/region of the case
- `Document type` — filing type attached to case (Petition, Decision, Opinion, Order, etc.)
- `Document date` — date of each filed document
