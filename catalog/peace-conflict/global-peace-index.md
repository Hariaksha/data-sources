# Global Peace Index (GPI)

**Theme:** Conflict & Safety
**Source:** Institute for Economics & Peace (IEP)
**Geographic Coverage:** Global (~163 countries)
**Time Coverage:** 2008 – present (annual)
**Unit of Observation:** Country-year
**Format:** PDF report, downloadable data
**Access:** [GPI 2025](https://www.economicsandpeace.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/GPI-2025-web.pdf)
**License:** Free to access
**Last Verified:** April 2026

---

## Description

The GPI ranks countries by their level of peacefulness using 23 indicators across three
domains: ongoing conflict, societal safety and security, and militarization. Produced annually
by the Institute for Economics & Peace.

## Key Variables

| Variable | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| GPI score | Overall peacefulness score (lower = more peaceful) |
| Ongoing conflict domain | Intensity of internal and external conflict |
| Safety & security domain | Crime, political terror, refugee flows |
| Militarization domain | Military expenditure, arms imports |

## Potential Research Questions

- Which countries are most at risk of conflict-climate feedback loops?
- How does peacefulness correlate with climate vulnerability across Southeast Asia?
- Longitudinal trends in conflict-affected countries targeted for development research

## Merge Keys & Compatibility

- Merge with **ACLED**, **CCVI**, and **Fragile States Index** via `iso3c` country code
- Consistent annual coverage makes it suitable for panel regressions

## Known Limitations

- Composite index — individual sub-indicators may tell a different story than the aggregate
- Definition of "peace" is contested; reflects IEP's normative framing
